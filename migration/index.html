Rover migration FAQ for sitters new to Rover

What is happening with DogVacay and Rover? On May 1, all United States DogVacay hosts and sitters were invited to migrate their profiles to Rover, which can be done with just a few clicks. You’ll still be able to manage existing bookings on DogVacay, but we encourage you to manage your business on Roverâ€”that way, all your info will live in one place.

After June 12, you can continue creating new bookings on Rover, but you will no longer be able to book on DogVacay.

  • Why are DogVacay and Rover coming together?

    Rover and DogVacay share the same mission: To make life better for dogs and the people who love them. We can make an even bigger impact together. This union will make it easier for pet parents to get trusted care for their dogs, for pet sitters to connect with more dog owners nationwide, and most importantly, for dogs to get the best neighborhood care.

  • What is Rover?

    Rover is the nation’s largest network of 5-star pet sitters and dog walkers. Similar to DogVacay, Rover connects pet parents with people who will treat their dogs like family. As a Rover sitter, you’ll still be able to choose your schedule, services, and rates, and continue to build your own pet-sitting business.
